The programme

Three phases, sequential, each measurable.

Each phase builds on the previous one. Phase 1 proves the campaign mechanic in the Dutch market. Phase 2 scales it into the UK alongside CuliQuiz's own launch. Phase 3 activates the organisation layer with Irish operators, with Bord Bia as the introducing partner.

Q3 — Q4 2026 · Pilot

  • Topic: Origin Green and Irish beef
  • Target reach: 5,000 unique players
  • Quarterly debrief and reporting pack

  • Foundation for further international rollout
Strategic alignment

Built around Bord Bia's 2025 — 2028 strategy.

The programme is designed to deliver measurable contributions to three core Bord Bia objectives. Each phase reports against these three lines.

1. Anchoring the product story with hospitality professionals. Origin Green, grass-based farming, animal welfare, flavour profile. Specific knowledge that needs daily refresh, delivered through game-based repetition rather than one-off workshops.

2. Deepening knowledge among foodservice professionals. Spirit of Ireland already proves there's appetite for educating hospitality staff about Irish products. The same model, scaled into a daily micro-learning flow covering beef, dairy, seafood and spirits.

3. Brand awareness with the next generation. Bord Bia's existing programmes target young people entering the food sector. CuliQuiz already serves that exact group, in vocational programmes and the first years of their careers — at scale, daily, measurably.

The UK is by some distance Ireland's largest food export market, and Bord Bia is on the ground in both Dublin and London. CuliQuiz active in NL/BE and ready to enter the UK lines up neatly with where you already work. Ireland itself is the natural follow: a smaller, focused home market where local foodservice operators can connect their teams directly to CuliQuiz as their own organisation on the platform.

Assumptions & risks

What we're banking on. And what could shift.

A few honest planning assumptions and the main risks worth naming up front. We'd rather lay these out clearly than hide them in the small print.

Content readiness. Bord Bia supplies the source material on Irish products (Origin Green, beef, dairy, seafood, spirits). CuliQuiz adapts it into microgames within 4 — 6 weeks per campaign.

UK launch timing. Phase 2 assumes CuliQuiz enters the UK market in early 2027. If timing slips, NL campaign cadence steps up and UK shifts to mid-2027.

Ireland operator commitment. Phase 3 assumes Bord Bia introduces at least one Irish foodservice operator willing to onboard. The first operator unlocks the rest; without one, Phase 3 becomes a campaign-only continuation rather than channel activation.

Procurement structure. Pricing assumes Bord Bia uses a market-activation budget on a per-campaign cost basis. If a different procurement structure is required (annual partnership fee, framework agreement), we restructure in the scoping workshop.

Reporting alignment. Quarterly reporting cycles assumed, matched to Bord Bia's own. Specific KPIs and report formats finalised together before pilot launch.
