Where we sit

A platform for hospitality professionals.

CuliQuiz is a microlearning platform for food, drinks and hospitality. Players are chefs, F&B staff, butchers, sommeliers and the students who'll be doing those jobs in the next five years. Today around 50,000 of them in the Netherlands, growing.

Three things that matter for an organisation like Bord Bia:

Audience

The people you want to reach

Hospitality professionals at every level: from culinary schools to senior chefs. The exact group that ends up telling guests where the beef, the cheese or the whiskey came from.

Behaviour

Daily, on the floor

Players play in short bursts of a few minutes a day. Hundreds of micro-touchpoints per person per year, instead of one annual training they forget about.

Measurable

Per location, per team

Who's learning what, where retention drops, which stories land. Useful in a market activation report, not just a pitch deck.

That's the engine. The next sections sketch where it could connect with what Bord Bia already does.

Where it lines up

Three places where Bord Bia's work meets ours.

Bord Bia already invests in product education for retailers and hospitality: butcher trainings, fresh-counter staff, the Spirit of Ireland programme. The challenge isn't the content, it's the channel. Workshops and PDFs land once and fade. The question is always whether the story still travels to the guest a month later.

Three of Bord Bia's stated objectives map directly onto what CuliQuiz does day-to-day:

1. Anchoring the product story with hospitality professionals. Origin Green, grass-based farming, animal welfare, flavour profile. Specific knowledge that needs daily refresh, exactly the kind of content built for game-based repetition.

2. Deepening knowledge among foodservice professionals. Spirit of Ireland already proves there's appetite for educating hospitality staff about Irish products. The same model works for beef, dairy and seafood, scaled into a daily micro-learning flow instead of one-off workshops.

3. Brand awareness with the next generation. Bord Bia runs programmes aimed at young people entering the food sector. CuliQuiz already serves that exact group, in vocational programmes and the first years of their careers.

International ambition

Not just the Netherlands.

CuliQuiz is built to scale across markets. Inbound interest from eight countries on five continents, before we spent a euro on growth. The UK and Ireland are next in line.

Active today

Netherlands and Belgium. Around 50,000 hospitality professionals on the platform.

Inbound interest

UK, USA, Australia, New Zealand, Austria, Morocco, Jordan and Thailand. Eight countries, five continents, zero marketing spend.

Why this matters for Bord Bia

The UK is by some distance Ireland's largest food export market, and Bord Bia is on the ground in both Dublin and London. CuliQuiz active in NL/BE and ready to enter the UK lines up neatly with where you already work. Ireland itself is the natural follow: a smaller, focused home market where local foodservice operators can run on a white-label CuliQuiz.

Two paths

A campaign, or a channel.

Two realistic ways to work together. One focused on the Dutch hospitality audience CuliQuiz already reaches. One opening a channel into Irish foodservice operators where Bord Bia already has relationships.

Path A · Campaign

Bord Bia as a content partner.

What we see

Bord Bia has the product story. CuliQuiz has the Dutch hospitality audience playing daily. The piece in between, structured content that travels from supplier to floor staff, is exactly what microlearning is good at.

What's included

  • A branded Bord Bia stream inside CuliQuiz
  • Microgames on Irish beef, dairy, seafood, spirits — built around Origin Green, sustainability, flavour
  • Daily 3 minutes on the phone, no extra training days for operators
  • Dashboard with reach per region, per role, per operator
  • Quarterly content refresh aligned with Bord Bia's own campaign calendar
  • Measurable reach, fits market-activation reporting cycles
Path B · Channel

CuliQuiz as white-label for Irish operators.

What we see

Bord Bia has working relationships with large Irish foodservice operators: hotel groups, catering companies, hospitality buyers. Those operators need scalable training tools. CuliQuiz can sit underneath, branded as theirs.

What's included

  • White-label CuliQuiz environment per operator
  • Bord Bia as the introducing partner into Ireland as a market
  • Content libraries on Irish ingredients available to every operator
  • Per-operator dashboards plus an aggregated view for Bord Bia
  • Foundation for international expansion: same engine, multiple markets
  • Co-funded setup, recurring per-seat or per-location pricing

CuliQuiz 2.0

What we're building next to it.

Under the hood we're working on the next layer: CuliQuiz 2.0. The direction isn't a bigger quiz app, it's a learning ecosystem.

Two foundations carry it: gamification as the engine that drives the learning, and the community as the place where players, brands and educators meet. Not a standalone app, but an environment that keeps running on its own.

For an organisation like Bord Bia that matters: you join where it already happens, instead of launching a separate platform that has to fight for attention every day.
