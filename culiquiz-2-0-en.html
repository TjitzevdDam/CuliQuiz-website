The foundation

One entity: the Organisation

No separate concepts like partner, tenant, restaurant or event. Everything is an Organisation. The difference sits in three dials: the type (what they are), the capabilities (what they may do) and the roles (what users may do). That keeps the architecture simple and endlessly scalable.

Four core entities, clearly linked

Users never belong directly to an organisation. The link always runs through a Membership. And every campaign has exactly one owner.

Analytics & privacy

Who sees which data

The key platform principle: the organisation owns individual data, the campaign publisher only gets aggregated insights. Privacy by design.

The organisation owns

Visible to Organization Admins
  • Employee & membership data
  • Individual progress & XP
  • Individual levels & mastery
  • Completion rates per employee
Hotel New York sees the XP and wine mastery of John, Sarah and Mike.

The campaign publisher owns

Visible to brands & partners
  • Campaign reach & participation
  • Completion & performance
  • Aggregated knowledge growth
  • Never traceable to one person
Heineken sees 300 organisations, 6,000 employees, 72% completion — but never John’s XP.
