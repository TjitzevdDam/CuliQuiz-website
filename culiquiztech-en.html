CuliQuiz, the tech story

A learning platform for hospitality, with a classification AI underneath.

CuliQuiz trains the largest workforce in the world, three to five minutes a day. Our Menu AI recognises every menu, classifies it and links it to a validated database that grows more valuable with every upload. We measure the effect in the point-of-sale system. Built entirely in-house, already profitable today, and around it something no pure software player has: a community and live events.

The concept

The missing layer, in software

Every corner of hospitality has a system. Hotels a PMS, restaurants a POS, marketing a CRM. For the knowledge and behaviour of the people on the floor, there is nothing. We fill that gap: a daily, gamified learning layer that keeps knowledge alive where it counts, at the table.

What Duolingo did for language, we build for craftsmanship in food, drinks and hospitality. Microgames of three to five minutes, multiple game formats, streaks and mastery per topic. Today around fifty thousand users in the Netherlands and profitable; worldwide from next year.

In one term: Gamification as a Service (GaaS). The game engine, the classification AI and the validated database as one platform that any organisation deploys as its training layer.

Data & privacy

Who sees which data

The core principle of the platform: the organisation owns the individual data, the brand or partner gets only aggregated insights. Privacy by design, and exactly what makes sell-out intelligence possible without identifying anyone.

Visible to the organisation
The organisation owns
  • Employee and membership data
  • Individual progress, XP and mastery
  • Completion per employee
A restaurant sees the wine mastery of its own team, by name.
Visible to brand / partner
The brand sees aggregated only
  • Reach and participation of the campaign
  • Completion and knowledge growth in the aggregate
  • With the POS integration: the effect on sales
A brand sees 300 venues, 6,000 employees, 72% completion, never one person.

This model delivers two things at once: brands get sell-out intelligence across the whole sector for the first time, and the employee keeps their privacy. That combination is the foundation under the revenue model, and hard to rebuild without the same data layer.
