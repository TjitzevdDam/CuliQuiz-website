What Worldchefs has built

A network few associations ever reach

Worldchefs has built something rare. A network of chefs across 110 countries, more than two hundred national associations and education partners, and an Academy that has welcomed over twenty-five thousand aspiring chefs in eight languages, with more than nineteen thousand graduating.

A partnership with the Electrolux Food Foundation through Feed the Planet, work with the FAO through the World Food Forum, and a biennial Congress that returns to Paris in 2028, a hundred years after Worldchefs began there. Few associations in any industry carry this much reach and this much mission at the same time.

What's still open

The layer in between

In everything we've looked at, one pattern stands out. Worldchefs is strong at bringing chefs into the profession. The Academy gets them to their first job. Certification recognises their growing skill. But the everyday layer in between, the daily reinforcement of craft after the formal training ends, is not something any ecosystem really covers. Knowledge fades within weeks. Managers spend years trying to keep it alive through briefings and product trainings, and most of it slips away anyway.

This is not a Worldchefs problem. It's an industry problem, and nobody has solved it at scale. It's the exact reason CuliQuiz exists.

What CuliQuiz brings

Ninety seconds a day

CuliQuiz is not a course. It's not an e-learning platform, and it's not a certification. It's a daily game that takes ninety seconds and keeps craft alive in the people who serve the guest. Wine, ingredients, technique, the story behind what's on the plate. The kind of knowledge that fades within weeks after training ends, and that managers spend years trying to hold onto.

We do for craft in food, drinks and hospitality what Duolingo did for language. Small daily moments, gamified, measurable, addictive in the right way.

Today we have around fifty thousand users across the Netherlands, partnerships with brands like Coca-Cola, Hendi, Rational, Tork and Mokka D'or, and a platform rebuild that lets us scale globally from September 2026. We are not asking Worldchefs to build anything. We're asking whether what we've already built fits where you want to take your community.

Three ways we could create value together

A flagship impact program with measurable scale, in line with your Feed the Planet work and your Electrolux partnership, fundable through routes you already know.

Timeline: twelve to eighteen months to a first funded cohort.

One honest correction

On the Gates Foundation

In our first conversation, the Gates Foundation came up as a possible funding route. We took that seriously and looked closely. We don't think it's the right door. Gates funds global health, agricultural development, and US education, almost always through proposals they invite themselves rather than open applications. A European program for culinary craft sits outside what they fund and outside how they fund it. We'd lose a year chasing a no.

What is realistic, and what we'd like to explore with you, is the Electrolux Food Foundation as the first call, because they're already in your orbit and they fund exactly this kind of work. Then European routes like Erasmus+ and EIT Food for the program-funded track.
